FEATURED PROJECTS

Hey! I'm Henry and I like to make stuff! I'm a bit of a jack of all trades and a master of none, but I really enjoy learning new skills and trying my best to create things worth consuming! Here are some projects I'm rather proud of!

Sun Clock

The Sun Clock project, which started simply as a final project for Object last semester, quickly turned into one of my all-time favorites. A group project with Quynh Tran and Nini Papunashvili, this piece of wall decor was my first venture into internet-enabled electronics as well as aesthetic physical fabrication.

At its core, the electronics are run by an Adafruit Feather Huzzah, which communicates via AdafruitIO to retrieve time data as well as sunrise and sunset data. Armed with this information, the array of NeoPixels can display various representations of the time and day/night cycle. It also has such features as a solid-color accent light and "party mode."

I am really proud of this project not because it changes anyone's life or accomplishes an incredible feat, but because I made it with my hands and it's pretty enough to hang in my apartment. It required a lot of time, patience and problem solving, but I learned a lot along the way!

"The Dap"

This personal project started in an attempt to jump onto the NFT craze train and make a lot of money, but I came up short on time and marketing skills.

The Dap was destined to be a randomly generated series of 10,000 renders, each depicting the same gentleman preparing to dap up an off-screen friend. Each render would depict a unique combination of clothing, wall art, accessories and body color. Material rarity in the collection made some more desirable than others.

This was the goal, and so I started to design. I drew inspiration for my character model from a tool called Character Creator, and designed all of the clothing from scratch in a program called Marvelous Designer. I began to design materials and lighting in Blender, and eventually began to achieve renders that I was happy with. I started learning a python scripting library for Blender to facilitate the random generation and rendering of the combinations, and I started to learn Solidity, a language I would use to write smart contracts on the Ethereum blockchain.

Unfortunately, I underestimated the amount of work that I was signing up for, and overestimated both my motivation and the free time I'd have after school and my job took priority.

The reason I still have decided to put this project on display here is because of how much I learned and how far I came in a project I truly had no business undertaking. Fashion design, character modeling and everything to do with blockchain technologies were all far outside my capabilities when I started, but I tried hard and put time into learning, and I'm happy with the results that I achieved.

Controller Overlays for Streamers

A long-term project of mine that stemmed out of personal necessity was my venture into professional, freelance design work, when I made custom "controller overlays" for gaming streamers.

I'm not ashamed to admit that, during COVID, loneliness and boredom drove me to try streaming my favorite videogame, Rocket League. Rocket League is a game where you play soccer with flying cars, in essence, but the practice and finesse required to achieve a high level is impressive. Many streamers choose to show what their hands are doing with the controller by showing a virtual one on screen, so that their viewers can both better understand the mechanics of the game and feel a more personal connection, in some ways.

I wanted a controller overlay and I wanted to make it my own, so I customized the design template to match my stream's aesthetic. It was terrible at first, of course, but rendition after rendition improved the results. After upgrading controllers to a new model, I found that there was no templates for the new model to use as a jumping off point. I made my own templates, and they were good. I wrote programs in Processinig to create realistic shadows and wrote tools to help warp designs to create accurate 3D perspective. I began creating high resolution animations and other features that were new to the market, and streamlined the installation process to make using my designs as easy as possible.

Alongside the technical improvements I made and design skills I developed, I was also successfully marketing my design services to other streamers. Quickly, I became the go-to guy for people looking for controller overlays, nearly worldwide. Gaming organizations, professional esports players, you name it!

I am really proud of myself for the work I did and the effort I put into improving my skills. While I am no longer active on the scene, I am happy that I could provide a service for people. I still host all my designs online and many are still used to this day, years later.

CNC Drawing Machine

This project is one that I hold in very high regard. Unlike the others on this page, I didn't create a standalone product with a life that ended when I decided it was done. Instead, I made a tool. In the height of my endeavours creating generative art with Processing and p5.js, I was tired of printers and I was tired of saving images to my computer by the thousands. My interest was piqued when I saw someone on Instagram using an AxiDraw pen plotter, and I decided to challenge myself to make my own.

The sad truth is that my drawing machine is a little too big to keep out in the apartment all the time, so it lives a lonely, purposeless existence behind some crap in my closet, where it is almost never used. One day it may reawaken. Who knows?

The construction was made mostly of aluminum extrusions, and a company, OpenBuilds, made it really easy to purchase parts that were designed to work with one another, for the most part. The circuitry for making an arduino control the stepper motors was a custom adventure where I'm sure I made everything more difficult than it had to be, but this was my first time upgrading a project from a breadboard, so I'm happy with the result.
